Abstract

Advanced technologies have transformed how accounting professionals handle data, making accounting increasingly a STEM-related field that requires strong data analytics and technology skills. This case study gives students hands-on experience using SQL and Tableau to perform real-world data analysis. It covers tasks like data extraction with SQL, data visualization using word clouds, geographic maps, and boxplots, data grouping through clustering, and communicating insights via interactive dashboards and storytelling. The goal is to help students appreciate how SQL and Tableau can be powerful tools for analyzing and visualizing large datasets, while developing a strong data analytics mindset.

Case Study

Dillard's, Inc. is a luxury department store chain headquartered in Little Rock, Arkansas, founded in 1938 by William T. Dillard. It expanded rapidly through acquisitions in the 1960s and became a public company in 1969. Today, it operates about 282 stores across 29 states, with Texas (57) and Florida (42) having the most locations, all still under family control.

The company has long collected daily transaction data from all stores. The CEO has tasked the cosmetics department manager with preparing a performance report analyzing key metrics — total sales, discount rate, profit margin, sales growth rate, and return rate — and to suggest one additional analysis. Using Microsoft SQL Server, the goal is to provide an insightful review of the Christmas season to help enhance the company's holiday sales strategy.

Project Summary: Dillard's Cosmetics Data Analysis

We analyzed Dillard's cosmetics data using SQL and Tableau. After preparing and cleaning the dataset, two new fields (Discount Rate and Profit Margin) were created.

Key analyses included:

  • Geographic Map: Compared state discount rates and profits across months.
  • Word Cloud: Highlighted top-selling brands in Florida and Louisiana with promotion suggestions.
  • Box Plots: Explored discount and sales distributions to check their relationship.
  • Clustering: Grouped products into "Sales Winners" and "Profit Winners" and identified outliers.
  • Sales Trends: Tracked monthly sales over years and forecasted next-year performance.
  • Return Analysis: Compared return rates with industry averages.
  • Story & Dashboard: Built an interactive Tableau dashboard and story to summarize insights.

Deliverable: A presentation with all visualizations and key insights.

Case Prerequisites

Students learned SQL basics and Tableau for data analysis. In week 11, they started the Dillard's case as a final individual project, submitted and presented in week 13. Pre- and post-tests showed clear improvement in SQL and Tableau skills. The case helps assess analytical abilities and can be used as a final or in-class project.

d) Geographic Map Analysis

Analysis of the discount level of states during the Christmas Season (December 2015 to January 2016):

Geographic map of state average discount rate for December 2015

The geographic map displays the state average discount rates of the cosmetic department for December 2015. The overall discount rate was relatively low in December, and the majority of states met the target discount rate of 5% for that month.

Geographic map of state average discount rate for January 2016

The majority of states offered more attractive discounts in January 2016, with only three states (Oklahoma, Arkansas, and Louisiana) meeting the target. The discount rate of Iowa experienced a significant jump, rising from 9.63% to 19.03%.

e) Word Cloud Analysis

Word cloud: total sales of cosmetic brands in Florida

The most popular cosmetic brands in Florida are Clinique, Estee Lauder, Lancome, Mac, and Chanel Women. The sales of these five brands significantly surpass those of other brands.

Word cloud: total sales of cosmetic brands in Louisiana

The most popular cosmetic brands in Louisiana are the same as Florida, but the sales difference between the top 5 brands and other brands is smaller in Louisiana.

f) Box Plot Analysis

Box-and-Whisker plots: state average discount rates per weekday

The majority of states meet the target of 5% excluding Iowa, with fluctuations mostly ranging between 0.7% and 3.7%. However, on Thursdays and Fridays, the discount rates are notably higher.

Box-and-whisker plots: state total sales per weekday

Despite significantly higher discount rates on Thursdays and Fridays, it did not result in higher sales on Thursdays. Sales on Saturdays are noticeably higher with lower discount rates.

g) Clustering Analysis

Clustering analysis: brand clusters
Clustering analysis: brand clusters (excluding the outlier)

Cluster Analysis:

  • Cluster 1: Profit Winners - highly profitable but low sales. Consider offering discounts or free samples.
  • Cluster 2: Sales Winners - most popular brands. Place in prominent locations and provide generous gifts instead of discounts.

h) Trend Analysis

Trend analysis of sales growth rate

Sales fluctuated significantly between January and August 2015, then increased rapidly after August until the end of the year. The decline was smoother in 2016 compared to 2015.

i) Return Rate Analysis

Return rate analysis

Dillard's cosmetic products' return rates are lower than the industry average in most months, excluding January. The higher return rates in January are understandable due to Christmas returns.

Return rate by tender types

The return rates of gift card transactions are much higher than other types of payments.

j) Dashboard

Dashboard analysis for Texas

The dashboard analysis for Texas shows that the most popular cosmetic brands are Clinique, Estee Lauder, Lancome, Mac, and Chanel for Women. Texas stores often offer substantial discounts on Fridays, but this may not significantly boost sales.
